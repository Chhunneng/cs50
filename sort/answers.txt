sort1 uses: Selection Sort

How do you know?: The poor performance on larger datasets, especially reversed or shuffled lists.

sort2 uses: Bubble Sort

How do you know?: The characteristics, such as quick performance on nearly sorted or reversed lists and a slower performance on larger randomly shuffled lists.

sort3 uses: Merge Sort

How do you know?: The characteristics, such as consistent performance regardless of the input order and a significant increase in execution time for larger input sizes.
